“The sourness of poverty mixes with the sweetness of discovering what makes this life worth a Brazilian classic.” - Booklist

“A near-complete view of life from the eyes of a child… has the power to warm and break hearts, no matter the age of the reader.”- The National

Get to know Zezé, the most adorable and super mischievous boy who dreams of growing up to be a bow tie poet. Not everyone will accept that "cute" thing.Because, in that poor suburban neighborhood, the surrounding austerity blinded people to the kind heart and wonderful imagination
of the five-year-old boy.

It doesn't matter how much rejection and scolding, because Zezé has a special friend to vent to: the sweet orange tree in the back garden. And also a friend, in the flesh, one day appeared, showed the sensitive boy early what love is, what is pain, and forever changed his life. Opening with
pure tones and ending in nostalgic low notes, My Sweet Orange Tree makes us realize that the true beauty of life comes from simple things like the white flower of the following tree. home, and that life is miserable without love and compassion. This classic book has therefore continuously made
the hearts of readers around the world sob, since its first release in 1968 in Brazil.
